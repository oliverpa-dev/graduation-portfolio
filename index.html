Summary

The graduation internship took place at Cape, a Tilburg-based software company specializing in Software-as-a-Service platforms. Cape is driven by a passion for innovative technologies that aim to enable marketers, designers, and media teams to unleash their full potential, allowing them to concentrate on what truly matters; the creation of more pertinent and high-performing campaigns. This project delves into the settings of the entire platform and its functionality. The main goal of the assignment is to create a user-friendly and scalable method for non-developers to independently create, edit, and manage settings. The desired solution includes a clickable interface that allows non-developers to manage the settings.

Introduction

Embarking on a transformative exploration of the Design Thinking methodology, this portfolio unfolds its narrative by immersing itself in the Empathize phase. Here, a deep dive into the current state of settings and the nuanced needs of the target audience lays the foundation for the subsequent phases. Transitioning to the Define phase, the portfolio distills key findings derived from meticulous observations and interviews into a concise and insightful summary.

The journey advances to the Ideate phase, where a panoramic view of settings management in diverse SaaS platforms unfolds. A meticulous examination of their content in the context of user experience and usability is accompanied by a journey through user flow and the conceptualization of initial sketches. Advancing smoothly, the storyline seamlessly shifts into the Prototype phase, where the wireframes are crafted, leading to the creation of a prototype.

In the Testing phase, a comprehensive review of usability analysis unfolds, prompting iterations through the prototype. Simultaneously, a MoSCoW analysis is crafted, delineating the elements earmarked for implementation in the interface and establishing their priorities.

Concluding with insights and counsel, the portfolio wraps up by offering an advice and a self-reflections based on the entire internship journey.

  • Conclusion from the result:

    The tool for managing campaigns utilizes various channels, including Display, Social, Video, Mobile, and Email, each of which has its own editable specifications within a campaign. These campaigns can encompass modules, markets, languages, brands, and departments, each serving distinct functionalities based on user permissions. The Multi market functionality allows for the assignment of different markets to a campaign, while languages determine the display language and offer automatic translation capabilities. User roles should be fixed for the customers with a space for creating a personalized role. This comprehensive understanding of the tool's components and features is essential for effective kick-off of the project plan.

Preferences for interaction include web forms and graphical interfaces, with flexibility and customization emphasized, but with the option to fall back to JSON. Concerns include backward compatibility and transitioning from JSON, while respondents also cited familiarity with other self-service tools and willingness to participate in usability testing for a new interface. Ideate

  • Question: How are the setup settings managed by other applications?
  • Research method: Market analysis
  • Why: To generate and develop new ideas.
  • How: By creating list of inspiring web applications and defining objectives, which aspects of settings management I am going to look for, then gather that information, pinpoint the advantages and user needs that appear to be unaddressed and, in the end, create a comparison.
  • Conclusion from the result:

    Each application, including Monday, Asana, Basecamp, Trello, and Jira, excels in distinct aspects of user interface design, providing user-friendly and efficient settings management experiences. Common strengths across these platforms include prioritizing clarity through clear labeling, icons, and intuitive layouts. They also demonstrate organized permissions, efficient team management, and modal-based workflows to enhance usability. However, differences arise in customization levels and navigation clarity, showcasing each app's unique balance between simplicity and functionality. Monday stands out for its clarity, clear labeling, and organized permissions. Asana's thoughtful design and dynamic presentation of sub-settings enhance user convenience, while Basecamp focuses on a simplified user experience but faces usability challenges in navigation. Trello excels with an intuitive modal interface and nested sub-settings, aided by a top indicator for user-friendly navigation. Jira combines user-friendly design with high customization options, providing efficient user management through structured tables and dropdown menus. Each application offers a tailored approach to meeting user needs in the realm of settings management.

User Flow

User flow of Monday

When exploring the main page, referred to as the Dashboard, users won't find a direct icon for accessing settings. Instead, a user-friendly approach requires clicking on the profile picture in the top right corner, revealing a modal with various redirecting links, including the option to access settings. This design choice optimizes user experience by seamlessly integrating icons with text labels. Upon selecting the settings link, a new page unfolds, presenting primary settings on the left sidebar and their corresponding content on the right side. These primary settings are meticulously explained, complemented by icon-text labels, and feature a distinctive background color upon selection, fostering an intuitive user experience. Effortless navigation through different settings and sub-settings is facilitated by a consistent design and layout maintained across each section. Each sub-setting is thoughtfully described, enhanced with visuals and text, contributing to a smooth and enriching user interaction. Read More

Prototype

Wireframe

In the course of the wireframing process, several iterations were undertaken to enhance the user interface and functionality. The initial wireframe highlighted areas of improvement, such as non-functional button colors and the need for better descriptions in various settings. The second iteration addressed concerns on the General Page, Business, Modules, and Users sections, emphasizing the importance of descriptive headlines and streamlined alignment for improved visual appeal and user experience. Specific suggestions were made for various settings, including adding descriptions for user roles and explanations for language settings. The third iteration introduced the concept of grouping modules to establish a visual hierarchy, contributing to a more organized and intuitive layout. This demonstrated a commitment to refining the wireframe based on user feedback. The fourth iteration focused on making options with sub-options editable and including module descriptions for a clearer understanding. Additionally, recommendations were made to combine management and publishing in the Channels section, streamlining the user interface further. In the fifth and final iteration before prototype creation, the wireframe incorporated the accumulated feedback, ensuring a high-fidelity representation of the refined design. The iterative process showcased a commitment to user-centric improvements, resulting in a wireframe that lays a solid foundation for the subsequent prototype development. Read More

Testing

Usability Testing

As a collaborative team, we utilized the Useberry platform to integrate targeted questions and user flows specific to the sections we designed. This enabled us to gather valuable feedback on our design choices. I took the lead in conducting usability testing on the dosage timeline screen flow and also performed A/B testing on the latest iterations of the prototype that I developed. Initially, we faced some challenges during the test deployment, which prompted me to make necessary adjustments to the prototype overnight, ensuring a smooth and effective testing experience the following day. This iterative testing process allowed us to gather valuable insights and refine our design to enhance its usability and user satisfaction. Read More

Usability Testing Analysis

In summary, the analysis of the testing sessions reveals a generally successful implementation of the addition of a new brand, department, and market, demonstrating seamless execution and a user-friendly approach. The introduction of a toggle for customization in Task 1 is acknowledged for providing flexibility. However, feedback suggests areas of improvement, particularly in Task 2, where the creation of a new user role highlighted the need for clearer navigation and a more intuitive presentation of roles and permissions. In Task 3, participants recommended streamlining the user interface by reconsidering the terminology used for "Modules." The overall sentiment across Tasks 4 to 8 indicates well-received functionalities, with minor challenges noted in locating options and a desire for more visual representations. User suggestions for merging Channel Management and Publishing, adding connection state indicators, and refining terminology collectively highlight opportunities for enhancing the platform's cohesion and user-friendliness. Participants generally rated their experience positively at a commendable level of 4, with some initial challenges, particularly related to Sidebar visibility, promptly addressed. These insights collectively offer valuable perspectives for refining the platform's clarity and terminology, contributing to an improved and more user-centric experience. Read More
